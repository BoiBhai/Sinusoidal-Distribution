Most densities in everyday use are unimodal and ``bell shaped". The instinctive choice for a bell-shape is the crest of a sine-curve. Even disregarding its elegant mathematical properties, this crest forms a visually natural candidate as a probability density function. \cite{gilbert1893moon} mentions the \sindist{} in its most well recognized form to study the distribution of moon-craters, although \cite{edwards2000sine} describes a brief history of the different forms in which Gilbert's proposed density has been written in the past, including in the works of \cite{lagrange1770memoire} and \cite{pearson1902systematic}.
